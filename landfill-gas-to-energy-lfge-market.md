<p><p>Landfill Gas-to-Energy (LFGE) refers to the process of capturing and converting methane gas produced from decomposing organic waste in landfills into usable energy. The LFGE market is divided into two primary types: thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies involve high-temperature processes like combustion, gasification, or pyrolysis to generate energy from landfill gas. In contrast, biochemical reactions utilize microbial processes to break down organic matter, producing biogas that can be refined into renewable natural gas or biofuels, contributing to sustainable energy solutions.</p></p>
