<p><p>The edible packaging film market encompasses several types, including protein, polysaccharides, lipids, and others. Protein-based films are derived from sources like gelatin and casein, offering excellent barrier properties. Polysaccharides, such as starch and cellulose, provide biodegradability and versatility. Lipid-based films, often made from fats and oils, offer moisture barrier protection and enhance shelf life. Other materials may include composite films or those incorporating natural additives, catering to specific food preservation needs and sustainability demands in the industry.</p></p>
