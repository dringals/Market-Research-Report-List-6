<p><p>Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy (CRT) is a specialized treatment for heart failure that involves the use of a device similar to a pacemaker. It aims to synchronize the contractions of the heart's ventricles to improve cardiac output and quality of life for patients with heart rhythm disorders, particularly those with a left bundle branch block. The CRT device sends electrical impulses to the heart muscles, enhancing the efficiency of heart contraction and alleviating symptoms associated with heart failure.</p><p>The CRT market is anticipated to experience robust growth, driven by the rising prevalence of heart failure, increasing awareness about the benefits of CRT, and advancements in technology. <p><p>Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy (CRT) is a treatment for heart failure that improves the heart's efficiency by synchronizing its contractions. The CRT market is divided into two main types: CRT-P, or CRT Pacemakers, which provide pacing to support heart function without defibrillation capabilities; and CRT-D, or CRT Defibrillators, which combine pacing with the ability to deliver shocks for life-threatening arrhythmias. Both types are designed to enhance the quality of life and reduce hospitalizations for heart failure patients.</p></p>
