<p><ul><li>Allogeneic Bone Marrow Transplant</li><li>Autologous Bone Marrow Transplant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bone marrow transplantation market comprises two primary types: allogeneic and autologous bone marrow transplants. Allogeneic bone marrow transplants involve the transfer of stem cells from a healthy donor to a recipient, often used for treating genetic disorders and certain cancers. In contrast, autologous bone marrow transplants involve harvesting and reinfusing the patient's own stem cells, commonly utilized in cases like lymphoma or multiple myeloma. Both methods are crucial for restoring blood cell production and improving patient outcomes post-treatment.</p></p>
