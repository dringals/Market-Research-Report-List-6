<p><p>The automotive V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) market consists primarily of two technologies: Dedicated Short-Range Communications (DSRC) and Cellular Vehicle-to-Everything (C-V2X). DSRC is based on wireless communication protocols for short-range interactions, enabling vehicles to communicate with each other and infrastructure for enhanced safety and traffic management. In contrast, C-V2X leverages existing cellular networks to facilitate broader connectivity, supporting real-time data exchange over greater distances. Both technologies aim to improve road safety, efficiency, and the overall driving experience.</p></p>
