<p><p>The live poultry vaccine market includes two primary types: injectable vaccines and spray vaccines. Injectable vaccines are administered via syringe, providing targeted immunity directly into the bloodstream, which is beneficial for controlling specific diseases in flocks. On the other hand, spray vaccines are dispersed as an aerosol, allowing for rapid vaccination of large groups, ensuring uniform coverage. Both methods aim to enhance the health and productivity of poultry by protecting against infectious diseases and boosting overall flock immunity.</p></p>
