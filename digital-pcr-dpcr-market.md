<p><p>Digital PCR (dPCR) is a highly sensitive technique used for quantifying DNA, utilizing discrete reactions to enhance precision in measuring low-abundance targets. The market is primarily segmented into Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR and Droplet Digital PCR (ddPCR). Digital Microfluidic Chip PCR employs microfluidic technology for automation and miniaturization, enabling efficient sample processing. In contrast, Droplet Digital PCR partitions the sample into thousands of droplets, allowing for highly accurate quantification through statistical analysis of individual reactions, making it especially valuable in research and clinical applications.</p></p>
