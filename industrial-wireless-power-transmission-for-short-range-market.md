<p><p>Industrial wireless power transmission for short-range applications primarily utilizes two key technologies: electromagnetic induction and magnetic field coupling. Electromagnetic induction involves transferring energy through coils, generating an alternating magnetic field to induce voltage in a nearby coil, making it effective for charging tools and equipment without direct connections. Magnetic field coupling, on the other hand, employs closely aligned coils to achieve efficient power transfer over short distances. Both technologies enhance operational flexibility and safety in industrial environments by reducing the need for physical wiring.</p></p>
