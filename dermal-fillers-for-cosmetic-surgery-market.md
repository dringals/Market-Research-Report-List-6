Absorbable fillers, like hyaluronic acid and collagen-based products, are temporary and naturally metabolized by the body, requiring repeat treatments. Non-absorbable fillers, often made from permanent materials like silicone, provide long-lasting results but carry a higher risk of complications. Each type serves distinct aesthetic needs, influencing patient choice and market dynamics in cosmetic surgery.</p></p>
