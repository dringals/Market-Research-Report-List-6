<p><p>Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) devices are categorized into two primary types: Single Junction (SI) GaAs and Tandem (SC) GaAs. SI GaAs devices feature a single layer of semiconductor material, providing efficient performance, particularly in high-frequency applications like RF and microwave communications. In contrast, SC GaAs devices consist of multiple junctions, enhancing energy conversion efficiency, making them ideal for photovoltaic applications and high-efficiency solar cells. Both types leverage GaAs's superior electron mobility and direct bandgap properties for advanced electronic and optoelectronic applications.</p></p>
