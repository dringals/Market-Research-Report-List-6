<p><p>Urban surveying airships come in two primary types: blimps and semi-rigid airships. Blimps are non-rigid, relying on lighter-than-air gases for lift, offering flexibility in maneuverability and a spacious interior for equipment. Semi-rigid airships have a partially inflated structure, providing enhanced stability and aerodynamics, making them suitable for more precise surveying tasks. Both types are utilized for mapping, traffic monitoring, and environmental assessments, combining aerial perspectives with advanced technology to gather valuable urban data efficiently.</p></p>
