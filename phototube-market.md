<p><ul><li>Gas</li><li>Vacuum</li><li>Electron Multiplier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The phototube market is categorized into three main types: gas-filled phototubes, vacuum phototubes, and electron multiplier phototubes. Gas-filled phototubes utilize gases to detect light, making them suitable for low-light applications. Vacuum phototubes operate in a vacuum environment, providing high sensitivity and fast response times, ideal for scientific and industrial uses. Electron multiplier phototubes amplify the signal generated by incoming photons, enabling the detection of very low light levels, commonly used in high-energy physics and fluorescence applications.</p></p>
