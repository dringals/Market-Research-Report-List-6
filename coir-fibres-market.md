<p><p>Coir fibers are derived from coconut husks and are categorized into two main types: brown fiber and white fiber. Brown fiber, extracted from mature coconuts, is coarser and widely used in applications like doormats, brushes, and erosion control. In contrast, white fiber comes from immature coconuts, is finer and softer, making it ideal for products such as upholstery and ropes. The distinct properties of each type cater to different market needs, influencing their demand and pricing.</p></p>
