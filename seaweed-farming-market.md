<p><p>Seaweed farming can be categorized into two main market types: artificial culture and mariculture. Artificial culture involves cultivating seaweeds in controlled environments, such as tanks or raceways, allowing for precise management of growth conditions. This method often leads to higher yields and better quality seaweeds. In contrast, mariculture refers to the cultivation of seaweeds in open coastal waters, utilizing natural marine ecosystems. This approach can promote biodiversity and sustainability but may face challenges from environmental factors and competition from wild seaweed.</p></p>
