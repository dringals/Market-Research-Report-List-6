<p><p>The Floating LNG Pretreatment market consists of several key technologies aimed at ensuring high-quality liquefied natural gas. Amine treating is used to remove carbon dioxide and hydrogen sulfide from natural gas. Membrane treating employs selective barriers to separate impurities, enhancing gas purity. Mercury removal systems capture harmful mercury compounds, crucial for preventing equipment damage. Oxygen removal techniques eliminate oxygen to prevent combustion risks and hydrate formation. Together, these methods optimize the LNG production process on floating facilities.</p></p>
