<p><p>Activated coal, commonly used for air and water purification, is processed through physical, chemical, or combined reactivation methods to restore its adsorptive capacity. Physical reactivation involves heating the coal at high temperatures in controlled environments, removing adsorbed substances. Chemical reactivation employs chemical agents to enhance regeneration efficiency, often requiring lower temperatures. The hybrid approach merges both methods, optimizing performance and cost. This versatility caters to various industries, driving demand for reactivated activated coal in environmental applications.</p></p>
