<p><p>The radiopharmaceuticals market is segmented into diagnostic and therapeutic radioisotopes. Diagnostic radioisotopes are used in imaging procedures, such as PET and SPECT scans, to visualize internal organs and detect diseases like cancer. Therapeutic radioisotopes, on the other hand, are utilized in targeted radiation therapies to treat conditions, primarily cancer, by delivering localized radiation to tumors. This division reflects the dual role of radiopharmaceuticals in both diagnosing medical conditions and providing treatment, contributing significantly to medical imaging and therapy advancements.</p></p>
