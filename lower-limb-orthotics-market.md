<p><ul><li>Dynamic Orthotics</li><li>Static Orthotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The lower limb orthotics market is primarily categorized into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing movement, often used for conditions requiring functional assistance during activity, such as gait training. In contrast, static orthotics are intended to immobilize or restrict motion, offering stability to injured or weakened limbs, and are commonly used post-surgery or for severe conditions. Both types aim to enhance mobility, reduce pain, and improve overall limb function.</p></p>
