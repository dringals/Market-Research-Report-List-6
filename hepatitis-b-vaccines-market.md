<p><p>Hepatitis B vaccines can be categorized into two main types based on their production methods: yeast-derived and CHO (Chinese Hamster Ovary) cell-derived. Yeast-derived vaccines utilize Saccharomyces cerevisiae to produce the vaccine components, offering advantages like lower production costs and ease of manufacturing. In contrast, CHO-derived vaccines are produced using mammalian cell lines, which can lead to more complex and higher-quality antigens. Both types are effective in inducing immunity against hepatitis B, catering to diverse market preferences and manufacturing capabilities.</p></p>
